The State bird of New York

One of the frist birds to return north each spring the easter bluebird was designated as the offical state bird of New York in 1970. It is a medium sized songbird, witha short tail, chunky body, a reddish-orange chest, and blue wings. Their diet mostly consists of insects and small fruits.
